About the Notes

The lecture notes cover fundamental concepts in Nuclear and Particle Physics, starting from historical models to advanced topics like the nuclear shell model, collective models, and the Standard Model of particle physics. Each lecture is presented in a detailed, structured format with derivations and key explanations.

What You'll Find Here:

  • Lectures: A complete set of digitalized lecture notes, organized chronologically, with all equations rendered in LaTeX for clarity.
  • Tutorial: A focused tutorial on key concepts, designed to reinforce understanding through explanations and practice problems.
  • Exam: A 50-mark conceptual and mathematical exam with multi-part questions to test your comprehensive understanding of the topics.
  • CSIR NET Style Questions: Practice questions designed to mimic the format and difficulty of the CSIR NET exam, complete with detailed, collapsible solutions for self-assessment.

Topics Covered

The notes delve into various core areas of Nuclear and Particle Physics, including:

  • Rutherford Scattering and the Discovery of the Nucleus
  • Nuclear Properties (Size, Charge Distribution, Spin, Parity)
  • Binding Energy and the Semi-Empirical Mass Formula
  • Nuclear Models (Liquid Drop Model, Fermi Gas Model, Shell Model)
  • Nuclear Decay Processes ($\alpha$, $\beta$ decay) and Stability
  • Nuclear Reactions and Fission
  • Collective Models (Vibrations and Rotations)
  • Properties of the Nuclear Force
  • Fundamental Particles and Interactions (Standard Model)
  • Conservation Laws (Baryon Number, Lepton Number, Strangeness, Isospin)
  • Quark Model and Hadron Classification
